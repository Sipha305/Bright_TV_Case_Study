 ---Project Context---
The objective of this analysis is to understand how users consume BrightTV content and identify levers that can increase subscriber growth. The data is sessionlevel viewership combined with user demographics, which allows us to look not just at activity, but at behaviour patterns that influence retention and growth.

 ---Usage Trends – Overview---
At a high level, user activity fluctuates significantly rather than showing a stable daily pattern. Engagement depth also varies, and viewing behaviour is strongly influenced by the time of day. These patterns suggest that growth challenges are less about reach and more about consistency and habit formation.

----Daily Viewership Trend---
This chart shows daily viewing sessions over time. The key point is the variability—usage spikes on some days and drops on others. This tells us that BrightTV is often used reactively, rather than as a daily habit. That creates an opportunity to stabilise engagement by influencing when and how users return.

---Age Distribution---
The user base is skewed towards older age groups. This is important because it affects content preferences, marketing tone, and engagement strategies. Growth efforts should be aligned to the expectations and viewing habits of these dominant age bands rather than assuming a uniformly young audience.

----Race Distribution---
The race distribution shows a broad and diverse audience base. This diversity is a strength—it allows BrightTV to position itself as an inclusive platform with wide content appeal. It also means that segmentation and targeted content can be effective without fragmenting the audience.

----Time of Day Usage---
Viewing is heavily concentrated in evening hours. This confirms that BrightTV is primarily a postwork, leisure platform. From a growth perspective, this means promotions, notifications, and content releases should be aligned to these peak windows rather than spread evenly across the day.

---Top 10 Channels---
A relatively small number of channels drive a large proportion of sessions. This is a classic concentration pattern. These channels are not just popular—they are strategic assets. Prioritising and promoting highperforming channels can improve engagement without increasing content spend.

---Usage by Province---
Viewership varies significantly by province. This indicates that a onesizefitsall national strategy may not be optimal. There is room for regional targeting—whether through marketing, content promotion, or partnerships—to better match local behaviour.

---Key Analysis---
Pulling the insights together, engagement is driven more by timing and content relevance than by raw user numbers. Certain content reliably drives deeper viewing, and lowusage days follow predictable patterns. These are behavioural signals we can act on, not structural limitations of the platform.

---Recommendations---
Based on the data, the most effective actions are to focus on early user engagement, promote highengagement content more deliberately, align campaigns to evening usage peaks, and apply different strategies on lowusage days instead of treating all days equally.

---Closing Remarks---
The key message is that BrightTV has growth potential within its existing user base. By using behavioural insights to improve timing, curation, and engagement depth, the platform can drive subscription growth before investing heavily in new content or acquisition.
